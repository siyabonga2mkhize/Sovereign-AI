## 👤 Persona 1: The Lab Manager
![Persona 1](/docs/Diagrams/Persona/Persona%201.png)

### Profile Specifications

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Name & Age** | Thandi Mthembu, 42 years old |
| **Role** | Computer Lab Manager at Durban University of Technology (DUT) |
| **Key Challenge** | Struggles to understand technical English data (like "idle draw" or "carbon intensity") and cannot pinpoint which specific computers are wasting electricity |
| **Primary Need** | Easy-to-understand alerts in her native language (isiZulu) to identify and act on per-computer energy waste immediately |
| **Language Preference** | isiZulu for complex alerts and daily summaries; English for basic technical setup |

### Goals & Motivations
- Reduce electricity costs for the university
- Keep the lab running smoothly for students
- Understand why the electricity bill is so high
- Be seen as competent and effective in her role

### Pain Points & Frustrations
- Receives utility data and system logs in English — struggles to understand technical terms
- Gets blamed for high electricity bills but cannot pinpoint the cause
- Cannot tell if waste is from Computer 4, Computer 17, or the air conditioner
- Has no way to monitor individual computer energy usage
- Existing tools (NovaVue, GreenX) only show building-level data

### Typical Tasks
- Checking the lab is open and functioning
- Reviewing monthly electricity bills
- Reporting issues to ICT/IS Department
- Ensuring computers are turned off at closing time

### User Scenario

> *"I get the utility data every month. It's all in English. There are words like 'wattage', 'idle draw', and 'carbon intensity'. I know the bill is too high, but I cannot tell if it's from Computer 4, Computer 17, or the air conditioner. I feel frustrated because I'm expected to manage costs, but the data is not in a language I understand."*

### How Our Solution Helps Thandi
- **Alerts in isiZulu:** She receives WhatsApp messages in her own language
- **Per-Computer Detection:** She knows exactly which computer is wasting energy
- **Actionable Information:** She can act immediately — *"Computer 14B is left on. Close it now."*
- **Simple Interface:** No electrical engineering knowledge required

### Success Metrics
- Understands 90%+ of alerts without asking for clarification
- Reduces lab electricity costs by 10-20% within 3 months
- Feels empowered and confident in her role

## 👤 Persona 3: The Student
![Persona 3](/docs/Diagrams/Persona/Persona%203.png)
### Profile Specifications

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Name & Age** | Zanele Zulu, 20 years old |
| **Role** | Second-year Information Systems student at DUT |
| **Key Challenge** | Frequently leaves computers idle or forgets to log off, completely unaware of how her actions directly impact energy waste and carbon emissions |
| **Primary Need** | Passive, accessible education and native-language on-screen reminders that empower her to adopt green habits |
| **Language Preference** | isiZulu for environmental awareness messages; English for academic materials |

### Goals & Motivations
- Complete assignments on time
- Access computers when she needs them
- Understand how her actions affect the environment
- Feel good about contributing to sustainability

### Pain Points & Frustrations
- Does not think about energy waste when leaving a computer idle
- Does not know her actions contribute to high electricity costs
- No awareness of the environmental impact of her computer usage
- Not educated on how to be energy-efficient

### Typical Tasks
- Using computers for assignments and research
- Studying in the lab with friends
- Printing documents and saving files
- Sometimes forgetting to log off or close the computer

### User Scenario

> *"I come to the lab to do my assignments. I don't think about turning the computer off when I leave. I didn't know that an idle computer wastes electricity and creates carbon emissions. If someone told me in a way I understood, I would make sure to close it."*

### How Our Solution Helps Zanele
- **Educational Messages:** On-screen reminders in isiZulu about energy waste
- **Awareness Campaigns:** Students become aware of their environmental impact
- **Sense of Purpose:** Students feel empowered to contribute to sustainability
- **Language Inclusion:** Students understand the message because it is in isiZulu

### Success Metrics
- 80%+ of students understand the importance of turning off computers
- Students actively choose to log off or close idle computers
- Students feel informed and empowered to contribute to sustainability

## 💡 Notes & Comments

### Important Considerations:

1. **Language is Critical:** Our personas clearly show that language (isiZulu) is a key differentiator. This is what makes our solution unique.

2. **Different Needs for Different Users:** Lab managers need immediate alerts; ICT/IS needs reports; students need education; Green Campus needs data; Deans need strategic stories.

3. **Low Barrier to Entry:** Our solution must work with minimal training — lab managers are busy and cannot spend hours learning a complex system.

4. **Measurable Impact:** Success must be measurable for each persona. For Thandi, it's understanding alerts. For Thabo, it's cost reduction. For Dr. Sipho, it's carbon reduction.

5. **Scalability:** The personas must work for multiple labs and eventually multiple universities.

## 📎 Appendix: Interview Template

### Interview Questions for Lab Staff:

1. What is your role and how long have you been doing it?
2. How do you currently monitor energy usage in the lab?
3. What language is the energy data typically in?
4. What do you find difficult to understand about the energy data?
5. Have you ever wanted to know which computer is wasting energy?
6. How would you prefer to receive alerts about energy waste?
7. What would help you reduce energy waste more effectively?
8. What language would you prefer for alerts and instructions?

### Interview Questions for ICT/IS:

1. What tools do you currently use to monitor computer labs?
2. What are the limitations of these tools?
3. How much do you spend on electricity for computer labs?
4. How important is per-computer monitoring for your work?
5. What compliance requirements (POPIA) affect your data collection?
